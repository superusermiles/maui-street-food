# Māui Street Food

## Business Snapshot
- **Business name:** Māui Street Food
- **Location:** Ponsonby, Auckland, New Zealand
- **Tagline:** Feed the night.
- **Business type:** Late-night street food truck collective and open-air dining yard

## What They Do
Māui Street Food is a loud, after-dark food truck collective built for people who are not ready to go home when the bars shut and the city still feels electric. It brings multiple street food vendors into one outdoor dining space in Ponsonby: smoke in the air, grills firing, bass in the background, neon glow on wet concrete, and a rotating mix of bold late-night eats. It is part food destination, part hangout, part event space. The experience should feel hot, social, and memorable before someone even sees the menu.

## Target Audience
- Night owls in their 20s and 30s who move between bars, gigs, and late dinners in central Auckland
- Pasifika and multicultural locals who want a food venue that feels rooted in street culture rather than polished hospitality clichés
- Groups of friends deciding where to eat after 9pm and wanting energy, variety, and atmosphere in one place
- Event-goers looking for pop-up nights, DJ sets, collabs, and reasons to come back beyond a single meal
- Visitors staying in or near Ponsonby who want a distinctly local, high-energy food experience instead of a generic restaurant strip

## Brand Voice
Loud, flavourful, unapologetic. The brand speaks with swagger and rhythm. It should feel street-smart, proud, and alive. Short lines. Punchy calls to action. No timid language. It blends urban edge with Pasifika pride, warmth, and generosity.

## Pages Needed
1. **Home** — Big first impression, core story, truck highlights, atmosphere, and key actions.
2. **The Trucks** — Showcase the resident food trucks, signature dishes, and what each one brings to the yard.
3. **Events** — Promote late-night events, DJ sets, collabs, and seasonal programming.
4. **Find Us** — Location, opening hours, map, transport notes, and a contact form for bookings and vendor/event enquiries.

## Specific Requirements from Client Brief
- 4-page site only: Home, The Trucks, Events, Find Us
- Brand personality must feel loud, flavourful, and unapologetic
- Visual direction should combine street culture with Pasifika pride
- Atmosphere should evoke neon signs, smoke clouds, and after-dark energy
- Include logo direction and favicon direction
- Include typography, color palette, page-by-page content structure, image direction, and implementation notes

## Creative Direction Summary
This site should not feel like a clean food hall brand. It should feel like a place you hear before you arrive. The visual language leans into deep charcoals, lava reds, acidic neon, and warm grill-fire highlights, balanced with confident typography and layered layouts that mimic posters, signage, and painted walls. The goal is instant appetite, instant atmosphere, and strong recall.
